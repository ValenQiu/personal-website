1. Background

This was a project I have done when I was participating the Mitacs Globalink Research Internship at the Toronto Metropolitan University in Canada, and I was the intern of the Robotics, Mechtronics & Automation Laboratory (RMAL).

The concept of a cooperative continuum robot (CCR) is similar to that of other cooperative robots. In a CCR system, there is an operative arm (OA) that carries out the main task, while the other arms are referred to as supportive arms (SAs), which assist and enhance the motion of the OA. The SAs play a crucial role in improving the performance of the OA, such as enhancing its stiffness and overall capabilities.

There was a few studies about cooperative continuum robots (CCRs) have been done. There are two main types of CCR: [1]

  • Parallel Supportive Type: Namely co-manipulative type, a few CRs grasp and co-manipulate an object in parallel or perform the planned task cooperatively.

    • Parallel Supportive Type

  • Serial Supportive Type: A group of supportive arms grasps and supports an operative arm in conducting the task. Since the supportive arms can access the work environment from different paths than the operative arm, a set of new applications can be established for continuum robots.

    • Serial Supportive Type

There was already a study about CCR in the RMAL. In the previous study, two catheters connected with a mount statically (fixed) which the two catheters are parallel at the connection point [2].

Cooperative Continuum Robots: Concept, Modeling, and Workspace Analysis

2. Concept

A Cooperative Continuum Robotic System with a supportive robot that can change its connecting point along the Operative robot.

  • Variable stiffness.
  • Adapt to more complex scenarios.

Design Goal:

  1. Two homogeneous Continuum Robots.
  2. An end-effector on the tip of the supportive robot that can “grasp” the body of the operative robot.

3. Design

3.1. Continnum Manipulator:

Single-Segment Tendon-Driven Continuum Robot:

  • Materials: Backbone: Nickel Titanium Alloy
  • Spacer Disks: PLA
  • Length: 400mm
  • Disk diameter: 25mm
  • Disk thickness: 2.5mm
  • Tendon displacement: 20mm

Driven by Dynamixel XL430-250.

3.2. Pneumatic Gripper:
The gripper is mounted on the tip of the operative arm, thus it is required to be:

  • Light weight as much as possible (19g)
  • A solid frame with two soft (silicone) actuators

Gripper Controller:
The gripper controller is inspired by V. W. Oguntosin's work [3].
