Background

DJI RoboMaster High School Robotics Camp is one of the best robotic competition for high school students in China. Every year, about 100 high school robotic competition participants will be selected from the whole nation and oversea, gethering for 21 days and finishing a challenging project.

Rules & Ideas

Each team occupies the sentry (50 points) by placing a ball (1 point) and a cup (6 points) on the sentry, and then scores by connecting the channel (10 points). The final score is obtained by weighting the automatic and manual time.

The sentry field consists of two distinct sides, designated as red and blue. Traditionally, robots are only permitted to place their own cups on their respective side. However, what if we could create a robot capable of placing a cup on our side from the opposing side? In this scenario, the rules impose strict limitations on size. Our team pondered the possibility of designing a robot that could effectively reduce the space beneath itself, allowing for the transfer of a mechanism clip to our side of the sentry from the opposite side, all while adhering to the size restrictions. It was essential to ensure that the robot remained stable throughout the process. If successful, this innovation had the potential to introduce an entirely novel robot to the Robomaster Camp, never witnessed before.

Dedicating numerous days to meticulous planning, we scrutinized every detail and rectified numerous mistakes along the way. Boldly embracing our imagination while exercising caution, we ultimately achieved our goal of constructing such a robot. During this summer camp, we demonstrated the feasibility of our design, providing evidence of its potential effectiveness. By pushing the boundaries of conventional thinking and combining our collective efforts, we successfully crafted a robot that defied expectations. This accomplishment validates our hard work and showcases the possibilities that arise when innovation and determination converge.

Design

Telescopic Frame: Replace the chassis with a frame
We expanded the chassis into a frame. A set of sliding rails were installed on both sides, which not only allowed the lower half to shrink, but also could withstand greater radial forces than the plain shafts. At the same time, the frame makde the robot highly integrated. To prevent the robot from stretching randomly, we installed a limit device. At the same time, the 2006 motors were used to drive the wheels, which not only makes the limiter force much smaller than the 3508 motors, but also because of the 2006 motors were equipped with the smaller size Mecanum wheels, making the lower space more retractable.

Ball Storage:
The limiter mechanism comprises a 3D printed quarter circle and a bezel, cleverly integrated with a ball storehouse. Initially, my intention was to design this limiter specifically for the upper ball storehouse. By utilizing a servo motor, the device effectively controlled the release of balls, allowing only one to be dispensed at a time. Later on, by combining three similar devices to serve as a limiter for the lower platform, and it accommodates and releases up to three balls simultaneously, while maintaining independent control for both sides. Through this iterative design process and collaborative effort, we successfully developed a versatile limiter mechanism that optimizes ball management for both the upper and lower sections of the system.
