Design

The basic structure of suspension geometry of the HKE-04E was push-rod suspension at the front, and rear suspension structure was pull-rod. The push-rod layout at front was beneficial to aerodynamics, which guided the air flow from the front wing properly towards the side-pods and the diffuser. As for the rear suspension, the pull-rod layout can provide a lower center of gravity that enhanced the cornering stability.

As for the tyre selection, Continental Formula Student Tire, competition tire 2016 (C16) was used, and the wheel diameter of 13” provide adequate room to prevent from packing problem.

The suspension system response to weight transfer of car is arranged best setting, and the geometry parameters such as change of camber, change of roll angle are designed to have a more desirable result. As for component design, it could achieve both the lightweight and high strength. Overall, the acceleration, cornering, braking and handling performance was enhanced.

An adjustable suspension system is beneficial to different riding conditions. The upright is equipped with an adjustable camber function so that it can modify the camber angle based on the track characteristic and the driver feedback. The anti-roll bar was designed to have multiple motion radio to produce different roll performance. The car can adjust understeer/oversteer balance during cornering, which it accomplishes by fine‐tuning the amount of load that transfers to the outside tires at the front versus the rear.

HKF-04E suspension was credit to the improved suspension geometry, the suspension force exerted to suspension components has significantly decreased, thus less material needed for 3D CAD designed components to withstand suspension force and ultimately can reduce the weight of the suspension system. Such weight reduction can provide quicker acceleration and more agile cornering ability. Therefore, reducing the mass of the racing car is suitable for the acceleration. (Total weight of suspension component:40.8 kg )

Different sensors are installed on the suspension system so that the instant data can be recorded, for example, damper displacement, rpm of wheels and the strain on anti-roll bar. After optimization the data, it can be found out the car performance such as roll rate, weight transfer, longitudinal acceleration etc. This recorded data is useful for analysis car dynamic and the next-generation improvement.

Indeed, racing team tasks are always a collective task and it is not only requiring each team member to work as much hard as they can but also need to collaborate closely to other members, it therefore prevents from design errors.
