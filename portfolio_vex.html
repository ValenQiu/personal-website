Details

Design Ideas & Background

VEX's new motor V5 was launched simultaneously with the start of the new season, but our school still used the previous generation of 393 motor, which has a large performance gap with V5. However, if I can make a robot with some effective functions based on the existing conditions, we can still defeat the opponent by cooperating with my teammate.

Rules

Major scoring methods include:
    A Toggled High Flag is worth 2 points.
    A Toggled Low Flag is worth 1 point.
    A High Scored Cap is worth 2 points.
    A Low Scored Cap is worth 1 point.
    A Robot Parks at alliance side earns 3 points,
      park at the center earns 6 points.
    An Alliance that wins the Autonomous Bonus earns 4 points.

Design

Chassis:
When making the chassis, I mainly thought in two aspects:How to maximize the use of space in the chassis and how to use the power of the motor more efficiently. In order to save space for installation of ejection, suction ball and other structures, I Place the motor in the center of the chassis and drive the wheels through a chain with a transmission ratio of 12:15 to achieve greater speed. To compete for berthing points, it is also important to reduce the kinetic energy of chassis collision losses. I installed a set of idler wheels on the front of the robot. When the robot collided with the parking surface, the front of the robot would be lifted up, making the robot come on the stage very fast.

Collection device:
It has a large width that can collect two balls at the same time and store them in the device. It is made by a belt with paddles, and driven by a 393 motor. The boll will be transfer to the device by the paddles, and guilded to the center. Then, the boll will be pushed upwards to the puncher. Meanwhile, it can store two bolls inside, and let the robot to carry them.
